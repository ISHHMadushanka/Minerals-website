Conserving Minerals

As standards of living rise around the world, there is greater demand for durable goods and products manufactured from mineral materials. As large emerging economies, such as China and India, increase their participation in the global economy, demand for critical mineral resources is increasing at a rapid rate. That means that we are depleting our known mineral deposits at an increasing rate, requiring that new deposits be found and put into production.

It takes millions of years for the formation of minerals. Compared to the present rate of consumption, the replenishment rate of minerals is very slow. Hence, mineral resources are finite and non-renewable. Due to this, it is important that we conserve the mineral resources.


Following steps should be taken for conservation of minerals :

1. Modern advanced technology should be used for mining operations. It will reduce wastage of resources in mining operation.

2. Metals like iron, copper, zinc, aluminium, etc. should be recycled for reuse.

3. The minerals available in very small quantity should be replaced with minerals available in sufficient quantity. for example use of aluminium in place of copper.

4. Alternative inexhaustiable resources of energy should be used in place exhaustiable sources of energy like hydro-electricity or solar energy should be used in place of petrol or diesel.

5. Synthetic products should replace scarce natural products.

6. Export of exhaustible minerals should be banned.
